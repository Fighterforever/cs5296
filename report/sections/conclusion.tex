\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We deployed an identical container image of a stateless URL-shortener
to AWS EC2, ECS Fargate, and AWS Lambda and benchmarked all three
under a controlled workload from a co-located k6 worker. EC2 keeps
the lowest tail latency at every measured load; Fargate offers the
best balance between elasticity and operational cost; Lambda absorbs
sudden bursts the fastest but pays a measurable cold-start tax that
matters mostly for the first request after an idle gap. Combining the
measured per-request latency with public April~2026 us-east-1 pricing
gives explicit break-even points (Lambda dominates Fargate up to
$\sim$17\,RPS and EC2 up to $\sim$60\,RPS for our 1\,GB workload).
The full pipeline --- Terraform, container build, k6 scenarios,
analysis, and figure generation --- is reproducible end-to-end inside
a standard personal AWS account with a single \texttt{make} target.
