\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We deployed an identical container image of a stateless URL-shortener
to AWS EC2, ECS Fargate, and AWS Lambda and benchmarked all three
under a controlled workload from a co-located k6 worker in the same
availability zone. In our measured regime Lambda delivered the
tightest tail (p95~$\approx$~40\,ms across 25--75\,RPS), a single
\texttt{t3.small} EC2 ASG held a clean 13\,ms median to 200\,RPS
before its burstable-credit tail widened, and a single 0.5\,vCPU
Fargate task could not absorb even 100\,RPS of Python work. Combining
the measured per-request latency with public April~2026 us-east-1
pricing gives a single break-even crossover at roughly 20\,RPS:
below it, Lambda's no-idle billing is cheapest; above it,
pre-provisioned compute wins. The full pipeline --- Terraform,
container build, k6 scenarios, analysis, and figure generation ---
reproduces every figure end-to-end on a standard personal AWS account
with a single \texttt{make} target.
