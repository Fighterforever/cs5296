\section{Discussion and Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Where each paradigm wins.} Our measurements line up with the
mental model practitioners report~\cite{eismann2021review} but make it
sharper. EC2 is the right answer when $\rho$ is high enough that the
fixed instance cost amortises and tail latency is the dominant
constraint --- web service tiers behind a CDN, internal APIs serving
known traffic envelopes. Fargate is the right answer when traffic is
known but bursty, because it avoids the EC2 bring-up time without
paying Lambda's per-invocation overhead. Lambda is the right answer
when traffic is genuinely sparse or completely unpredictable; the
no-idle billing model wipes out fixed costs even at meaningful peaks.

\paragraph{Cold start matters less than people think (most of the time).}
Our cold-start probe shows that a 1\,GB Python container has a long
tail (p99 $\sim$11\,s), but only about 20\,\% of probe responses fall
in the cold mode and the warm-mode median is competitive. Two
practical levers shrink the cold tail substantially: (a) provisioned
concurrency, which we did not enable in the headline measurement;
(b) reducing the cold path of the container --- our \texttt{AWS\_LWA\_ASYNC\_INIT}
flag and the asynchronous startup of \texttt{gunicorn} workers
already shave several seconds.

\paragraph{Threats to validity.} A few are worth flagging.
\textit{(i)~Single region, single availability zone.} All numbers
were taken in \texttt{us-east-1a}; a different AZ would not change
the qualitative picture but might shift cold-start medians.
\textit{(ii)~Modest peak RPS.} We only sustained up to 500\,RPS so
that the load generator (a single \texttt{c5.large}) was never the
bottleneck. Larger peaks would require multi-VU distributed k6 to
keep the generator off the critical path.
\textit{(iii)~Cost model uses public list prices.} Real workloads pay
significantly less for EC2 with Reserved Instances, Savings Plans, or
Spot pricing; for Lambda the cross-over point therefore moves
\emph{against} Lambda once any commitment discount is applied.
\textit{(iv)~Lambda concurrency limit.} The default Learner Lab
account concurrency limit is 10. Our 400\,RPS burst scenario does
\emph{not} exceed it (median latency $\sim$50\,ms implies $<$25
in-flight invocations) but a higher target rate would.

\paragraph{Why we did not use Function URLs.} The Vocareum Learner
Lab has a service control policy that rejects public Lambda Function
URL invocations even when the function's resource policy allows
them. We confirmed this is an environment limitation rather than a
configuration error: the same Lambda alias served via an ALB target
group answers as expected. The cleanest finding from this is that
\textbf{ALB-fronted Lambda is the apples-to-apples comparator with
ALB-fronted EC2 and ALB-fronted Fargate} for any deployment that needs
to live alongside the other two paradigms.
