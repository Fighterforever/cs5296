\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Comparative measurements of cloud compute paradigms are a long-running
thread. Wang et al.~\cite{peeking-serverless} characterised FaaS
runtime behaviour and exposed wide variation in cold-start times across
vendors and runtime sizes; McGrath and Brenner~\cite{mcgrath-faas}
proposed pre-warming based on workload prediction.
Eismann et al.~\cite{eismann2021review} systematise serverless
performance studies and identify a recurring weakness: the comparison
points are rarely identical applications. Our use of a single OCI
image across three runtimes via the AWS Lambda Web
Adapter~\cite{lambda-web-adapter} directly addresses that critique.
For cost analysis, Adzic and Chatley~\cite{adzic-serverless} examined
the conditions under which serverless wins over EC2 for a Java
workload at MindMup; their break-even RPS is in the same order of
magnitude as ours but for very different $\overline{t}$. Cordingly et
al.~\cite{cordingly2020lambda} ran a measurement study of Lambda's
billing model that informed our choice of 1\,GB rather than the
128\,MB minimum.
